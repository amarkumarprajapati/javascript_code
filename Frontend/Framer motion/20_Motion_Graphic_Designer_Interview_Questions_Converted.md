Are you a motion graphics designer with an upcoming job interview? If so, chances are
you’re feeling the pressure to make sure you shine and stand out from other candidates.
The good news is that there are some common questions asked in motion graphic
design interviews. By preparing answers to these questions ahead of time, you can give
yourself a leg up over the competition and show off your skills as a top-notch motion
graphic designer. Read on for some of the most frequently asked motion graphic design
interview questions—and how to answer them.
1. What is your experience with motion graphics software such as
Adobe After Effects, Cinema 4D, or Maya?
Motion graphics designers use specialized software to create dynamic visuals that can be
used in video, broadcast, and digital media. Knowing how to use these tools is essential
to being a successful motion graphics designer, so it’s important that interviewers ask this
question to get an idea of the applicant’s experience and proficiency with these
programs.
How to Answer:
To answer this question, you should talk about the motion graphics software that you are
most familiar with. Talk about any projects you have completed using the software and
how long you have been using it. If you don’t have much experience with the tools
mentioned in the job description, emphasize your willingness to learn and any other
relevant skills or experience you may have.

2. Describe a time when you had to create an animation from
scratch.
Motion graphic designers are expected to be able to take basic concepts and turn them
into compelling visuals. This question is designed to determine how well you can go
from concept to finished product in a timely manner. The interviewer will want to know
about your creative process, how you manage your time, and how you handle feedback
from a client or supervisor.
How to Answer:
Be prepared to go into detail about your experience with each software you’ve used,
such as how long you’ve been using it and what projects you’ve worked on. You should
also be able to explain the steps you take when creating motion graphics and how you
troubleshoot any issues that arise. Demonstrate your ability to work quickly and efficiently
by giving examples of time management strategies you use. Finally, emphasize your
willingness to accept feedback from clients or supervisors in order to create the best
product possible.
Example: “I recently completed a project for ABC Design Agency where I had to create
an animation from scratch. To start, I researched the company’s brand and created a
storyboard that outlined the main points of the video. Once approved by the client, I
used Adobe After Effects to bring the motion graphic to life. Throughout the process, I
was in constant communication with the client to ensure that their vision was being met. I
also took advantage of my experience with 3D software such as Cinema 4D to add depth
to certain parts of the animation. In the end, I was able to deliver a high-quality product
within the given timeline and exceeded the expectations of the client.”

4. Are you familiar with the principles of animation and how they
can be used to create engaging visuals?
Motion graphic design is a complex field that requires a thorough understanding of
animation principles and techniques. An interviewer will want to know if you have the
necessary skills to create compelling visuals that will engage viewers. By asking this
question, they can assess your knowledge and experience in this area and determine if
you’re the right candidate for the job.
How to Answer:
To answer this question, you should be prepared to talk about the principles of animation
and how they can be used in motion graphic design. You should also discuss any
experience that you have with using these principles in your work. Make sure to provide

visuals. Additionally, mention any software or tools that you’ve used to create motion
graphics. This will demonstrate your technical proficiency and show the interviewer that
you understand the fundamentals of motion graphic design.

used to create engaging visuals. I’ve worked on numerous motion graphic projects, using
software such as After Effects and Premiere Pro to create dynamic videos with special

applied the principles of squash and stretch to add realism to the character animations.
Additionally, I used keyframing techniques to create smooth camera movements and
make sure that the visuals were visually appealing.”
5. Explain your understanding of color theory and how it applies
to motion graphics design.
Color theory is an important concept in motion graphics design, as it helps to create
visuals that are eye-catching and effective. By asking this question, the interviewer is
looking to see how well you understand the fundamentals of design and how you use
color to create an impact. Your answer should demonstrate your knowledge of the basics
of color theory, as well as how you use it to create motion graphics that are visually
appealing and impactful.

models and textures.
Motion graphic design jobs often require the designer to create 3D models and textures.
This question is a way for the interviewer to assess your skills in this area. They want to
know if you understand how to use 3D modeling and texture mapping software, if you
can create realistic lighting and textures, and if you are familiar with the tools and
techniques needed to create a high-quality 3D animation.
How to Answer:
To answer this question, you should provide a brief overview of a project that required
you to work with 3D models and textures. Describe the software used, the techniques
employed, and any challenges you faced while working on the project. Be sure to
emphasize your knowledge of 3D modeling and texture mapping, as well as how you
overcame any difficulties encountered during production. Finally, mention the end result
of the project and any feedback or recognition you received for it.

results. They even featured the animation on their website and social media channels.”

8. What strategies do you use to make sure your animations are
visually appealing and effective?
Motion graphics are a combination of art and science. They have to look good, but also
be optimized for the medium they’re being used in. From color choice to timing, the
interviewer will want to know that you understand the nuances of motion graphic design
and how to optimize your animations for their intended audience.
How to Answer:
Start by talking about the motion graphic design process you use. This should include
researching the target audience, understanding their needs and preferences, and
choosing colors and fonts that will be visually appealing to them. Talk about how you
structure your animations for maximum impact—you want to make sure they’re not too
long or too short, and that the timing is just right. Finally, discuss any optimization
techniques you may use, such as compressing files or adjusting frame rates, to ensure
that your animations look great no matter what device they’re viewed on.
Example: “I always start by researching the target audience and understanding who they
are and what they’re looking for. I then use this information to choose colors and fonts
that are visually appealing to them. When it comes to structuring my animations, I want to
make sure they’re not too long or too short and that the timing is just right. I also use
optimization techniques such as compressing files or adjusting frame rates to ensure that
my animations look great no matter what device they’re viewed on. All of these strategies
help ensure that my animations are visually appealing and effective.”

13. What techniques do you use to keep track of multiple versions
of an animation?
Motion graphics designers have to be able to keep track of multiple versions of a project.
This could involve different versions of the same animation, as well as versions with
different animations. This is a critical skill for any motion graphics designer, and the
interviewer wants to know that you’re up to the task.
How to Answer:
To answer this question, you should describe the techniques that you use to keep track of
multiple versions. This could include using version control software like Git, or naming
conventions for different versions. You might also discuss how you organize files and
folders so that it’s easy to find the right version quickly. Finally, don’t forget to mention
any tools that you use to help automate the process, such as automated backups or
scripts.
Example: “I use a combination of techniques to keep track of multiple versions of an
animation. I use version control software like Git to keep a record of the changes I make
to the project, and I also use naming conventions for different versions so that I can easily
tell them apart. I also organize my files and folders so that it’s easy to find the right
version quickly. To help automate the process, I use automated backups and scripts to
ensure that I always have the latest version available.”

15. What strategies do you use to collaborate effectively with
other designers and developers?
Motion graphic design is a collaborative effort, and you need to be able to work with
other designers, developers, and other stakeholders to create the best product. The
interviewer wants to know that you can communicate and work effectively with others,
and that you understand the importance of collaboration and teamwork in motion
graphic design.
How to Answer:
Start by talking about how you approach collaboration. Do you prefer to take charge and
lead the project, or do you like to discuss ideas with other members of the team before
making decisions? Talk about the strategies that you use to ensure everyone’s opinions

facilitate collaboration, such as video conferencing software or task management
systems.

team members. I’m always open to feedback and I think it’s important to be as flexible as

for the project with clear deadlines so that everyone is aware of their responsibilities.”

users into your designs to make them more usable.

which performs better. I also make sure to use labels that are easy to understand, provide
