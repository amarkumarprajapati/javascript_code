3D Fundamentals:

Understanding 3D space, coordinates, and how to represent objects in a 3D environment.
Knowledge of 3D modeling concepts, including vertices, faces, and meshes.

Scene, Camera, and Renderer:

A Three.js scene represents a 3D space where objects are placed and rendered.
Cameras define the viewpoint of the scene, such as perspective or orthographic cameras.
The renderer is responsible for rendering the 3D scene to a 2D canvas on the web page.

Geometries and Materials:

Geometries define the shape of 3D objects, such as cubes, spheres, or custom models.
Materials define how the surface of objects appears, including textures, colors, and shaders.

Lighting:

Understanding different types of lights in Three.js, including ambient, directional, point, and spotlights.
Knowing how to set up and configure lighting in a scene to create realistic 3D visuals.

Textures and UV Mapping:

Applying textures to objects using UV mapping to make surfaces look more realistic.
How to load and apply images or videos as textures.

Animation:

Creating animations of 3D objects, including transformations, rotations, and scaling.
Knowledge of keyframe animations and animation libraries like Tween.js for smoother animations.

Interactivity:

Adding interactivity to 3D scenes, such as handling mouse and touch events.
Understanding raycasting for object picking and interaction.

Performance Optimization:

Techniques for optimizing the performance of your 3D scenes, like using object culling and LOD (Level of Detail).
Efficiently managing resources, including textures and 3D models.

Exporting and Importing 3D Models:

How to import 3D models created in 3D modeling software like Blender, Maya, or 3ds Max into Three.js.
Exporting models in formats like OBJ, FBX, or glTF.

Shaders and Post-Processing:

Knowledge of GLSL shaders for custom materials and visual effects.
Implementing post-processing effects like bloom, depth of field, and shaders.

Physics and Simulations (Optional):

Integrating physics engines like Cannon.js or Ammo.js for realistic physics simulations.
Simulating physical interactions like collisions, gravity, and constraints.

VR and AR (Optional):

Working with WebXR to create Virtual Reality (VR) and Augmented Reality (AR) experiences in the browser.
Understanding WebVR and WebAR APIs for device compatibility.

Responsive Design:

Creating responsive 3D applications that work well on various screen sizes and devices.

Debugging and Troubleshooting:

Using browser developer tools for debugging and diagnosing issues in your Three.js applications.
Finding and fixing common problems related to materials, textures, and geometry.

Community and Documentation:

Leveraging the Three.js community and its extensive documentation, examples, and forums for help and learning resources.
