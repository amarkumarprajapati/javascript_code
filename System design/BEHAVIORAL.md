# Behavioral / HR Interview Prep

> For 3+ years experience. Interviewers test communication, ownership, and culture fit. Prepare **stories**, not generic answers.

## STAR method
Answer behavioral questions in 4 parts:
- **S**ituation — context.
- **T**ask — your responsibility.
- **A**ction — what YOU did (use "I", be specific).
- **R**esult — outcome + metrics + learning.

## "Tell me about yourself" (60-second pitch)
Structure: **Present → Past → Future**.
> "I'm a full-stack developer with 3+ years building MERN applications. Currently I work on [X] where I [impact/metric]. Before that I [relevant experience]. I'm now looking for a role where I can [goal aligned with this company]."
- [ ] Write yours and rehearse out loud.

## Prepare these stories (2–3 each, reusable across questions)
- [ ] A challenging bug you solved.
- [ ] A project you're proud of (your impact).
- [ ] A conflict with a teammate / how you resolved it.
- [ ] A time you failed / missed a deadline + what you learned.
- [ ] A time you took ownership / led without authority.
- [ ] Handling tight deadlines / pressure.
- [ ] Receiving critical feedback.
- [ ] Mentoring a junior / helping the team.

## Common behavioral questions
1. Tell me about yourself.
2. Why are you leaving / looking for a change?
3. Why this company / role?
4. Greatest strength / weakness (pick a real, improving weakness).
5. Describe a difficult technical challenge.
6. Tell me about a time you disagreed with a manager.
7. How do you handle conflicting priorities?
8. A time you went above and beyond.
9. Where do you see yourself in 3–5 years?
10. How do you keep your skills updated?

## Project deep-dive prep (interviewers WILL drill your resume)
For each project be ready to explain:
- [ ] Problem it solved + your role.
- [ ] Architecture & tech choices (and **why**).
- [ ] Hardest technical challenge + solution.
- [ ] Scale (users, data, traffic).
- [ ] What you'd improve / do differently.

## Questions to ask the interviewer (always have 3–4)
- What does success look like in this role in 6 months?
- What's the team structure & code review/deployment process?
- What are the biggest technical challenges the team faces?
- How is the engineering culture (testing, tech debt, on-call)?
- What's the growth path?

## Salary / negotiation tips
- Research market range for your YOE + location.
- Give a range, anchor slightly high, justify with skills/impact.
- Don't disclose current salary first if avoidable; focus on value.

## Red flags to avoid
- Speaking negatively about past employers.
- Vague answers without specifics/metrics.
- Taking sole credit for team work (or none for yours).
- Not having questions to ask.
